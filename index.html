Something I hear a lot from software engineers is: “𝘐’𝘷𝘦 𝘩𝘦𝘢𝘳𝘥 𝘰𝘧 Monumental and seen your job postings — but I wasn’t sure if I’m the right fit...”

The hesitation makes sense; building software for robots does sound niche. It’s a bit more complicated than SaaS; there are more constraints, but in the end — it’s still just software engineering.

The challenges Monumental solves combine hardware, software, and infrastructure at scale — all while maintaining high availability on construction sites.

𝗔𝘁𝗿𝗶𝘂𝗺 , our OS for software-defined construction, lets us plan, deploy, simulate, and optimise everything from wall designs to managing robot operations. It integrates CAD, ERP, robotics control, and fleet management — enabling us to analyse results, investigate failures, and refine our builds in the real world and simulation.

We’re hiring software engineers who want to build full-stack software that interacts with the physical world.
